Having completed the foundational learning phase and moved into project implementation, I want to take this opportunity to reflect on my internship journey so far. This is a valuable chance to objectively assess my newly acquired skills and identify areas that need continuous improvement.

In the beginning, navigating AWS network setups and security constraints proved challenging. However, through persistent documentation research and weekly hands-on practice, I have gradually mastered the deployment workflows and developed a more structured architectural mindset. Nevertheless, I recognize that this is only the starting point, and I must push myself further to tackle more complex engineering challenges.

### Areas of Progress

After a period of experience, I feel that I have made clear progress in both mindset and practical skills:

* **Mastering AWS configuration workflows:** From only holding dry theories, I have personally set up a secure VPC network system, configured API Gateway connected with Lambda, created DynamoDB database, and deployed VPC Endpoints to optimize costs when connecting to S3.
* **Developing a troubleshooting mindset:** Whenever the system encounters connection or permission issues, I no longer panic but have learned to actively check security layers like Security Group, Route Table, IAM Policy, as well as read logs on CloudWatch to find the exact root cause.
* **Cultivating self-study methods:** By maintaining a serious learning roadmap, I have formed the habit of proactively reading technical documentation, studying official vendor Whitepapers, and always testing thoroughly at a small scale before actual application.
* **Consciousness of cloud resource control:** I understand very well how misconfiguration or forgetting to turn off services on cloud will affect costs. Therefore, I am always conscious of reviewing and cleaning up testing resources to avoid wasting the budget.
* **Improving writing and communication skills:** Maintaining a daily worklog and sharing weekly technical blogs has significantly improved my expression, knowing how to organize and convey complex technology information in a coherent, easy-to-understand manner.

### Areas for Improvement

Beside the achieved results, I realize that I still have some weaknesses and need to focus on improving them in the near future:

* **Overall architecture design skills:** I am still a bit confused when standing before a large problem that requires proposing an infrastructure architecture solution from scratch without prior documentation or guide.
* **IaC (Infrastructure as Code) proficiency:** Although I know how to write basic CloudFormation templates, I feel I still rely quite a bit on clicking manually on the AWS Console. My upcoming goal is to fully transition to managing infrastructure using code.
* **Advanced networking knowledge:** More complex networking concepts such as Transit Gateway or Route 53 Resolver in a Hybrid Cloud model remain a major challenge, requiring me to spend more time for deep and thorough research.
* **Automated CI/CD integration experience:** I have not had many opportunities for practical hands-on experience in setting up a complete automated pipeline to deploy products professionally from staging to production environments.
* **Time management skills:** I need to learn how to allocate time scientifically and with more discipline to balance well between completing school assignments and the progress of performing internship practical exercises.

### Summary

Looking back at the completed phases, I have successfully transitioned from "understanding service concepts" to "independent technical execution and troubleshooting." This foundation is critical as I focus on bridging my remaining technical gaps during the upcoming project phases.
